What do you care what other people think

What do you care what other people think 是 Richard P. Feynman 的一本自述，但是这个标题让我困惑了，怎么理解，“what do you care”这个说法？ what do you care 和 do you care what 看起来都没什么问题，但是 what do you care what 这样该怎么断句怎么理解？

这里其实我脑子不确定了一下，觉得 care 是不及物动词，所以翻了下词典，也是有及物属性的，如下:

transitive verb

to be concerned about or to the extent of <don’t care what they say> <doesn’t care a damn>

查了一下 “what do you care” 出来的对比用法是 “why do you care”，并且:

Why do you care? is employed as a real question: someone is concerned about something which seems innocent or trivial to you, and you ask for a reason. It is ordinarily spoken with emphasis on why and care (but see snailboat’s comment, below).

What do you care? is employed dismissively: it implies that the concern ‘you’ express is not something which touches ‘you’, and therefore ‘you’ should not meddle in the matter. It is spoken with emphasis on you.

也就是说，这其实是 “Why do you care what other people think” 的结构，表达“你为什么在乎别人怎么想”的意思，第二个 what 引导 care 的宾语。只不过跟中文里面“你知道什么”（问问题）和“你知道个什么”（带有贬驳意味）有一点相似，也像是“你为什么这么做”和“你何必如此”的差异，因而或许翻译理解为“你何必在乎别人怎么想”更为贴切。

但是很多网友也指出 “Don’t use very often because it is very rude, but that also depends in how you say it. Just be careful”，联系到这个文本上，目前猜测书名是这么“刚”的一句话，可能是因为，自传嘛，而且 Feynman 之前也写过 Surely you’re joking, Mr.Feynman 这种略有自嘲意味的书名，所以或许，在这里反而不是很 rude，反而更像是一种自黑和诙谐？
